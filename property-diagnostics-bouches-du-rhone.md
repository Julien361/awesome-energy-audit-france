# Property Diagnostics in Southern France: A Complete Guide for Buyers and Landlords

## What Are Property Diagnostics?

In France, real estate transactions and rental agreements require a series of mandatory technical diagnostics (*diagnostics immobiliers*). These assessments evaluate health, safety, and energy performance aspects of a property before any sale or rental contract is signed.

## Mandatory Diagnostics for Sales

For property sales in 2026, the following diagnostics are required:

- **DPE** (Diagnostic de Performance Énergétique): Energy rating from A to G
- **Amiante** (Asbestos): Mandatory for buildings built before July 1997
- **Plomb** (Lead - CREP): For pre-1949 buildings
- **Termites**: Required in declared infestation zones (most of Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur)
- **Gaz & Électricité**: For installations over 15 years old
- **Assainissement non collectif**: For properties not connected to public sewage
- **ERP** (État des Risques et Pollutions): Required everywhere

## Specifics for Bouches-du-Rhône (13)

The Bouches-du-Rhône department has specific requirements due to:

1. **Termite risk zones**: Marseille, Aix-en-Provence, and most municipalities in the Bouches-du-Rhône are classified in high-risk termite zones. The termite diagnostic is therefore almost always mandatory.
2. **Natural risk exposure**: The ERP (formerly ERNMT) is particularly detailed in this area due to wildfire risk, flooding (Rhône, Huveaune), and seismic activity.
3. **Lead in older housing stock**: Marseille has significant pre-1949 housing stock requiring systematic CREP.

## Finding a Certified Diagnostician in Marseille and Provence

All property diagnosticians must be certified by an accredited body (COFRAC) and hold liability insurance. The certification is specific to each type of diagnostic and must be renewed every 5 years.

For certified property diagnostics in the Bouches-du-Rhône, [diagnostic-immobilier13.fr](https://diagnostic-immobilier13.fr) provides a network of certified professionals covering Marseille, Aix-en-Provence, Aubagne, and surrounding areas.

## New DPE Rules Impacting the Provence Market

Since 2025, G-rated properties cannot be offered for new rentals. This has created significant pressure in the Marseille rental market, where older buildings with poor energy performance now require mandatory renovation before reletting. Landlords must calculate their renovation investment against the increased rental yield from improved DPE ratings.
