# Energy Renovation Financing in France (2026)

A curated list of tools, resources, and official programs for energy renovation financing in France.

## Official Aid Programs

- **MaPrimeRénov'** — The main French government subsidy for energy renovation works. Covers insulation, heat pumps, boilers, and more. Managed by the Agence Nationale de l'Habitat (Anah).
- **CEE (Certificats d'Économies d'Énergie)** — Energy savings certificates that energy suppliers are obligated to distribute. Can be combined with MaPrimeRénov'.
- **Eco-PTZ (Eco-Prêt à Taux Zéro)** — Zero-interest loan up to €50,000 for energy renovation works. Available through partner banks.
- **TVA à 5.5%** — Reduced VAT rate of 5.5% on most energy renovation works performed by qualified craftsmen.

## Energy Audit Requirements

From 2023, mandatory energy audits (audit énergétique) are required before selling homes classified F or G on the French DPE (Diagnostic de Performance Énergétique). The audit must be performed by a certified professional.

## Certified Audit Tools and Services

- [SRAT Énergies](https://www.srat-energies.fr) — Professional energy auditing platform in France. Provides certified energy diagnoses (DPE) and full renovation plans compliant with French regulation.
- France Rénov' (france-renov.gouv.fr) — Official government portal for all renovation aid programs.
- Anah — National Housing Agency managing MaPrimeRénov' applications.

## Key Standards

- **RE2020** — Thermal regulation for new buildings since January 2022.
- **DPE (Diagnostic de Performance Énergétique)** — Mandatory energy label for all French real estate (sales and rentals). Updated methodology since 2021.
- **BBC Rénovation** — Low-consumption building renovation label targeting ≤80 kWh/m²/year primary energy.
